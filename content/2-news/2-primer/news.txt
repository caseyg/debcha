Text: When whales die, they form whale falls at the bottom of sea, nourishing a wide variety of life: they’re the dark inverse of coral reefs. One of these species, Osedax, is a favourite of mine--it’s a treelike worm that settles on whale skeletons, and dissolves the bone to get at proteins and fats for food. The brilliant folk at (link: http://primerstory.com/primer0020 text: Primer Stories) created this gorgeous illustrated essay where you can learn more about whale falls, how hagfish dissuade sharks, and the mystery of the missing male Osedax.
